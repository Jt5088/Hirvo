[Researcher-2] [Top 3 Picks] -- Here are my nominations for the final 8, with reasoning:

[Researcher-2] [Pick 1: Jake's Resume (LaTeX)] -- This MUST be in the final 8. It is the most-used tech resume template in existence (~5k GitHub stars, 100k+ Overleaf uses). Single-column, zero decoration, 11pt Computer Modern, 0.5in margins. The design philosophy is "content IS the design." It proves that extreme minimalism works -- tight 1.0 line spacing, thin horizontal rules as dividers, right-aligned dates. ATS score is perfect. For Hirvo's template builder, this represents the "engineering standard" archetype that a huge segment of users will expect. Without it, the product feels incomplete.

[Researcher-2] [Pick 2: Swiss/International Typographic Style] -- This is my highest-conviction pick for MODERN aesthetics. The Swiss approach uses light-weight name headings (300-400 weight instead of bold), ALL CAPS section headings with 0.1-0.15em letter-spacing, generous 1.4-1.5 line height, and a mathematical grid system. It feels timeless rather than trendy. The 30/70 column split (narrow dates left, content right) creates natural reading rhythm. Whitespace ratio is 35-40% -- the highest of any template I analyzed. This is the template that makes a Hirvo user feel like they hired a designer. It also bridges modern and clean categories.

[Researcher-2] [Pick 3: Rezi Standard ATS] -- This represents the "optimized for function" end of the spectrum. Tightest margins (0.5-0.75in), densest content packing (25-28% whitespace), and verified ATS compatibility as its primary design goal. Thin heading underlines are the only decoration. This matters because many Hirvo users will prioritize "fitting more content" and "guaranteed ATS pass" over aesthetics. Having this in the final 8 ensures we cover the full minimalism spectrum: Swiss (aesthetic minimalism) to Rezi (functional minimalism) to Jake's (canonical minimalism).

[Researcher-2] [Key Insight: Spacing Sweet Spots] -- Across all 6 templates, I found consistent patterns. The sweet spots are: page margins 0.6-0.75in, section gaps 18-24pt, entry gaps 10-12pt, bullet spacing 4-6pt, line spacing 1.1-1.15, body text 10.5-11pt, names 20-24pt, headings 12-14pt. These ranges should inform Hirvo's default template values. Every strong template I analyzed creates hierarchy through type scale and weight, NOT decoration. Single-column dominates (5 of 6 templates). Horizontal rules are the universal section separator. Right-aligned dates are the standard pattern for timeline display.

[Researcher-2] [Design Principle] -- The single most important finding: "tight body text, generous section gaps." Every good minimalist template uses tight line spacing (1.0-1.15) within entries but generous vertical space (18-24pt) between sections. This creates scannable structure while maximizing content density. Hirvo's template system should encode this ratio as a core principle.
