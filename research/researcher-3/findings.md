## Template 1: Awesome CV (LaTeX / Overleaf)

**Source:** GitHub (posquit0/Awesome-CV), available on Overleaf
**Style category:** Smooth / Perfecto
**ATS compatibility:** High -- single-column, standard fonts, no graphics in parseable areas

### Layout Structure
- Single-column, full-width layout
- Clean header with name, contact info, and social links in a horizontal row
- Sections flow top-to-bottom: Summary, Experience, Education, Skills, Projects
- No sidebar -- all content occupies the full page width
- Color accent bar beneath the name provides the only decorative element

### Typography
- **Font family:** Source Sans Pro (primary), Font Awesome for icons
- **Name:** ~28-32pt, extra-bold weight (800)
- **Section headings:** 14pt, bold, uppercase with letter-spacing ~0.08em
- **Entry titles (job title):** 14pt, bold
- **Entry position/company:** 10pt, regular
- **Entry dates:** 8pt, right-aligned, lighter color
- **Entry location:** 7.8pt, italic
- **Body/description:** 9-10pt, regular weight
- **Typographic hierarchy:** 4 clear levels -- name > section heading > entry title > body text

### Spacing Analysis
- **Page margins:** Left 1.4cm, Top 0.8cm, Right 1.4cm, Bottom 1.8cm (tight but breathable)
- **Section spacing:** ~12-16pt between sections (controlled via \vspace{-2mm} adjustments)
- **Entry spacing:** ~8pt between individual entries
- **Line height:** ~1.2-1.3 for body text (LaTeX default with minor adjustments)
- **Bullet indentation:** ~12pt from left margin of entry
- **Date alignment:** Right-justified, creating a clean axis on the right edge
- **Section heading underline:** Thin 0.5pt rule spanning full width, ~4pt below heading text

### Visual Elements
- Single accent color (customizable -- default is a teal/cyan) used ONLY for: name, section heading rules, and icon highlights
- Section headings have a full-width thin underline rule
- Font Awesome icons for contact info (phone, email, GitHub, LinkedIn) -- small, inline, not boxed
- No backgrounds, no boxes, no cards -- purely typographic
- The accent color rule under headings creates rhythm without clutter

### What Makes It Premium
- The mathematical precision of LaTeX typesetting -- every character is perfectly kerned
- The restraint: one accent color, one decorative element (the heading rule), zero gimmicks
- Entry layout uses a 4-part grid: title | company | date | location -- all aligned on invisible axes
- The tight top margin (0.8cm) maximizes content area without feeling cramped
- Source Sans Pro is a Google-friendly, screen-optimized sans-serif that reads cleanly at small sizes

### What Makes It Premium

- Helvetica at the right size and spacing is timeless -- it will never look dated

## Cross-Template Analysis: Common Patterns in Premium Resume Design

### The Premium Typography Formula
1. **Name:** 18-32pt, heaviest weight available
2. **Section headings:** 12-16pt, bold or semi-bold, often uppercase with letter-spacing 0.05-0.12em
3. **Entry titles:** 11-14pt, bold
4. **Body text:** 9.5-11pt, regular weight
5. **Dates/secondary info:** 8-10pt, regular weight, often lighter color or right-aligned

### The Premium Spacing Formula
1. **Margins:** 0.6-1.0 inch (narrower = modern/dense, wider = editorial/luxury)
2. **Section gaps:** 14-28pt (the biggest variable across templates)
3. **Entry gaps:** 8-16pt
4. **Line height body:** 1.15-1.5 (tighter = dense/executive, looser = editorial/elegant)
5. **Bullet spacing:** 4-8pt between items

### What Separates "Premium" from "Standard"
- **Font pairing** (serif + sans, or weights of the same family) vs single font at one weight
- **Intentional whitespace** -- space that serves a purpose, not just "gaps"
- **Consistent alignment axes** -- invisible vertical and horizontal lines that everything snaps to
- **Restraint** -- one accent color, one decorative technique, zero visual noise
- **Hierarchy clarity** -- 3-4 distinct levels, never ambiguous
- **Typography as decoration** -- using weight, spacing, case, and size instead of icons/borders/boxes

### ATS Safety Principles (Across All Premium Templates)
- Single-column layouts parse most reliably
- Standard fonts (system fonts, Google Fonts) never cause issues
- Section headings should use standard labels (Experience, Education, Skills)
- Thin lines/rules are safe; complex graphics, tables, and multi-column parsing depends on the ATS
- PDF export from well-structured documents preserves reading order
- No headers/footers for critical content -- some ATS skip these areas

## Template 7: Standard Resume "Cordova" (EB Garamond + Inter)

**Source:** standardresume.co (Creative templates)
**Style category:** Elegant / Creative-ATS
**ATS compatibility:** High -- single-column flow, standard Google Fonts, no graphics in parseable areas, recruiter-reviewed design

### Layout Structure
- Single-column with a "minimalist with striking typography" approach
- The serif headings create natural section breaks without needing rules or dividers
- Standard resume sections supported: Contact, Summary, Experience, Education, Skills, plus custom sections
- Editable color palette allows customization without breaking structure
- Supports functional, chronological, reverse-chronological, and combination resume formats

### Typography
- **Font pairing:** EB Garamond (headings) + Inter (body) -- high-contrast serif/sans-serif pairing
- **EB Garamond characteristics:** Designed by Georg Duffner, a faithful digitization of Claude Garamond's original 16th-century typeface. High stroke contrast, elegant curves, slightly condensed proportions
- **Inter characteristics:** Designed by Rasmus Andersson, tall x-height for screen readability, extensive OpenType features including contextual alternates
- **Name:** ~22-28pt, EB Garamond, bold or semi-bold
- **Section headings:** ~14-16pt, EB Garamond, medium or semi-bold weight
- **Entry titles:** ~11pt, Inter, semi-bold (600)
- **Body text:** ~10-10.5pt, Inter, regular (400)
- **Dates/secondary:** ~9.5-10pt, Inter, regular, lighter color
- **The pairing philosophy:** EB Garamond's high stroke contrast and classic proportions against Inter's geometric neutrality creates a tension between "historical authority" and "modern clarity"

### Spacing Analysis
- **Page margins:** ~0.7-0.85 inch (Standard Resume's auto-spacing system optimizes based on content length)
- **Section gaps:** ~18-24pt between major sections
- **Entry gaps:** ~10-14pt between entries
- **Line height:** ~1.35-1.45 for body text (Inter's tall x-height allows slightly tighter leading)
- **Heading to content gap:** ~8-10pt
- **Bullet spacing:** ~5-7pt between bullets
- **Character count per line:** Targets 75-85 characters (per Standard Resume's handbook)
- **Automatic spacing adjustment:** Standard Resume's slider automatically adjusts margins and vertical spacing to optimal values

### Visual Elements
- Multiple text colors used strategically to guide reader attention -- not a single black, but a considered palette of dark gray tones
- The EB Garamond headings are the primary decorative element -- their calligraphic quality replaces the need for rules or borders
- Editable color palette (default appears to use warm orange-red gradient tones in branding, but resume itself uses restrained neutrals)
- No icons, no skill bars, no graphics
- Section separation achieved through the font contrast itself -- the jump from Inter body text to EB Garamond heading creates a natural visual break

### What Makes It Premium
- EB Garamond is the "connoisseur's Garamond" -- it references the original source material rather than derivative versions like Adobe Garamond or ITC Garamond
- The serif/sans pairing is the most visually distinct of any template in our research -- it creates genuine typographic contrast, not just weight variation
- Inter's OpenType contextual alternates adjust punctuation shape based on surrounding glyphs, creating micro-level polish that recruiters feel but cannot name
- The template bridges creative and professional: a designer would submit it confidently, and a banker would not be embarrassed by it
- EB Garamond at heading sizes (14-16pt) is where the font truly sings -- the stroke contrast becomes visible and adds richness that sans-serif headings cannot achieve
- This is the only template in our entire collection that uses a historically-sourced serif for headings -- it occupies a unique position in the design spectrum

## Template 8: Butterick's Practical Typography Resume (Equity + Concourse)

**Source:** Matthew Butterick's Practical Typography (practicaltypography.com), with LaTeX implementation at GitHub (thnatiw/edelgas-resume)
**Style category:** Typographer's Perfecto / Editorial
**ATS compatibility:** High -- single-column, standard PDF output, no graphics, no complex layout structures

### Layout Structure
- Single-column, one-page format (two pages acceptable for experienced candidates)
- De-emphasized section headings -- Butterick's philosophy inverts the typical resume hierarchy by making employer/school names the primary visual anchor, not section labels
- Information-dense but never cramped -- the space is earned through typography, not whitespace alone
- Header with name and contact details, followed by experience, education, skills
- Content-first philosophy: "most important information on page one"

### Typography
- **Font pairing:** Equity (serif, for body text) + Concourse (sans-serif, for headings/labels) -- both designed by Matthew Butterick
- **Equity characteristics:** A text serif designed for extended reading at small sizes. Clear letterforms, moderate stroke contrast, designed to replace Times New Roman in professional documents. Works well at 10-12pt.
- **Concourse characteristics:** A sans-serif companion to Equity. Geometric but warm, designed to pair with Equity without visual conflict. Used for headings, labels, and navigational elements.
- **Body text:** 10-12pt, Equity, regular weight
- **Section headings:** Concourse, likely semi-bold, deliberately smaller than typical resume headings -- Butterick recommends de-emphasizing these
- **Name:** Larger Concourse or Equity, but not dramatically oversized
- **Key principle:** "Gentler list bullets" -- standard bullet points replaced with lighter typographic markers
- **ATS-safe alternatives:** Helvetica/Arial can substitute for Concourse; Palatino/Book Antiqua for Equity

### Spacing Analysis (from Butterick's Practical Typography rules)
- **Page margins:** Larger than typical resume margins -- Butterick explicitly recommends "bigger page margins" than default. Likely 0.85-1.0 inch or more
- **Line height:** 120-145% of point size (for 11pt body text, that means ~13.2-15.95pt leading)
- **Line length:** 45-90 characters per line including spaces (Butterick's universal rule)
- **Space between paragraphs:** 4-10pt
- **First-line indents:** 1-4x the point size of the text (11-44pt for 11pt body)
- **Letter-spacing on caps:** 5-12% extra tracking on all-caps and small-caps text
- **Overall density:** Lower than engineering templates (Jake's, Awesome CV) but higher than executive templates -- the "literary professional" density level

### Visual Elements
- Zero decorative elements -- no rules, no lines, no color, no icons
- Custom letterspacing built into the Equity and Concourse fonts themselves -- Butterick baked optimal tracking into the font design
- Gentle list bullets instead of heavy circular bullets
- The visual identity comes entirely from the typeface quality -- Equity and Concourse have more personality than system fonts
- No colored elements -- pure black on white
- Emphasis through weight and size only, never through color or decoration

### What Makes It Premium
- This is the resume a professional typographer would make for themselves -- it is the theoretical ideal
- Butterick's fonts are purpose-built for professional documents. They have personality without being distracting, which is the exact quality that separates "premium" from "generic"
- The inverted hierarchy (employer names > section headings) is a genuinely original approach that makes the resume scan faster for recruiters
- The spacing rules (120-145% leading, 45-90 char line length) are not arbitrary -- they are backed by centuries of typographic research
- The "gentler bullets" detail seems minor but it changes the page texture -- the resume reads as a document, not a list
- For Hirvo, the principles matter more than the exact fonts: any quality serif + sans pairing at these spacing ratios will produce the same "typographer's resume" feel
- Equity and Concourse are paid fonts ($119 each), which means most users have never seen this style -- implementing it in Hirvo would be genuinely novel

## Round 2 Cross-Template Analysis: The Serif Renaissance

### Why Serif Headings Matter for Hirvo

All three Round 2 templates feature serif headings paired with sans-serif body text. This is not coincidental -- it reflects a broader design trend:

1. **Differentiation:** In a world where 90%+ of resumes use all-sans-serif typography, a serif heading immediately stands out
2. **Authority signaling:** Serif fonts carry historical associations with publishing, law, academia, and authority -- they signal gravitas
3. **Visual contrast:** The serif/sans split creates genuine typographic contrast that aids scanning, unlike weight-only hierarchies
4. **ATS safety:** Modern ATS systems parse text content, not font rendering. Serif fonts parse identically to sans-serif

### The Three Serif Approaches Compared

| Aspect | Cordova (EB Garamond) | Butterick (Equity) | Seymore (DM Serif Display) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Serif type | Old-style (Garamond) | Transitional (custom) | Transitional (display) |
| Visual weight | Medium -- elegant, refined | Medium-low -- restrained | High -- dramatic, commanding |
| Best for | Creative professionals, academics | Writers, typographers, lawyers | Editorial, design, marketing |
| Personality | "Quietly sophisticated" | "Deliberately understated" | "Confidently bold" |
| Body font | Inter | Equity (serif) or substitute | Inter |
| Cost | Free (Google Fonts) | Paid ($119 each) | Free (Google Fonts) |
| Heading contrast | High (serif vs sans) | Medium (serif vs sans) | Very high (display serif vs sans) |

### Implications for Hirvo Template Builder

The serif-heading approach should be one of Hirvo's template categories. The three templates represent increasing levels of visual drama:
- Cordova = "elegant serif" (safe for any industry)
